Email Routing with Cloudflare: Free Custom Email for Your Domain

Luzerge Team March 14, 2026 4 min read

If you are still sending emails from a generic Gmail or Yahoo address, your business is leaving credibility on the table. An email from info@yourdomain.com signals professionalism and trust. An email from yourbusiness2024@gmail.com signals that you are not serious enough to invest in your own brand.

The good news? You don't need to pay for email hosting to get a custom domain email. Cloudflare Email Routing lets you create unlimited custom addresses and forward them to your existing Gmail or Outlook inbox — completely free.

Why You Need a Custom Email Address

Your email address is often the first thing a potential client or customer sees. It appears on invoices, business cards, contact forms, and every piece of correspondence you send. Consider the difference:

  • info@yourbusiness.com — professional, trustworthy, established
  • yourbusiness.inquiries@gmail.com — informal, forgettable, amateur

A custom domain email does more than look professional. It reinforces your brand with every message you send, makes your business easier to remember, and builds trust with clients who may be comparing you to competitors. For businesses in the Philippines, where word-of-mouth and personal trust drive transactions, that credibility matters even more.

Custom email addresses also give you flexibility. You can create dedicated addresses for different purposes — support@ for customer service, billing@ for invoices, hello@ for general inquiries — all routing to the same inbox or to different team members.

What Is Cloudflare Email Routing?

Cloudflare Email Routing is a free feature available to any domain managed through Cloudflare. It works by intercepting emails sent to your custom addresses and forwarding them to one or more destination email accounts that you already own, such as Gmail or Outlook.

Here is what makes it compelling:

  • Completely free — no monthly fees, no per-address charges, included on every Cloudflare plan
  • Unlimited custom addresses — create as many as you need without additional cost
  • No separate email server required — no need to manage mailboxes, storage, or spam filters
  • Instant setup — configure everything from the Cloudflare dashboard in minutes
  • Catch-all routing — forward every possible address on your domain to a single inbox
Think of Cloudflare Email Routing as a mail forwarding service for your domain. Emails arrive at your custom address and get delivered to your personal inbox.

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Setting up Cloudflare Email Routing takes less than ten minutes. Here is how to do it:

1. Navigate to Email Routing in Cloudflare

Log into your Cloudflare dashboard, select the domain you want to configure, and click on Email Routing in the left sidebar. If this is your first time, Cloudflare will prompt you to enable the feature and add the required DNS records automatically.

2. Add Your Destination Email

Before creating custom addresses, you need to tell Cloudflare where to forward emails. Click Destination addresses and add your personal email — this is the Gmail, Outlook, or other inbox where you want to receive forwarded messages. You can add multiple destination addresses if different team members need to receive emails from different custom addresses.

3. Verify Your Destination Email

Cloudflare will send a verification email to each destination address you add. Check your inbox (and spam folder), click the verification link, and the address will be confirmed. This step ensures that only you can forward emails to your inbox — preventing abuse.

4. Create Custom Addresses

Now the fun part. Go to the Email Routing rules tab and click Create address. Enter the prefix you want (such as info, support, hello, or sales) and select which destination address should receive the forwarded emails. Click save, and the address is live immediately.

Common addresses to create for a business:

  • info@yourdomain.com — general inquiries
  • support@yourdomain.com — customer service
  • hello@yourdomain.com — a friendly alternative to info@
  • billing@yourdomain.com — invoices and payment inquiries
  • admin@yourdomain.com — administrative and account-related emails

5. Configure Catch-All Routing

A catch-all rule forwards emails sent to any address on your domain — even ones you haven't explicitly created. This is useful because it means typos or creative guesses from customers will still reach you. To enable it, go to the Catch-all address section and set the action to forward to your preferred destination.

Managing Routing Rules

Once your email routing is configured, you can manage it entirely from the Cloudflare dashboard. You can add new addresses, change which destination receives forwarded mail, disable specific routes temporarily, or delete addresses you no longer need. Each rule can be toggled on or off individually, giving you full control over your email flow without touching any DNS records after the initial setup.

If you have multiple team members, you can route different addresses to different people. For example, support@ goes to your customer service lead while billing@ goes to your accountant. Reorganizing is as simple as updating the destination in Cloudflare.

Limitations to Know About

Cloudflare Email Routing is powerful for what it does, but it is important to understand what it does not do:

  • Forwarding only — Cloudflare handles inbound email. It does not provide an outbound SMTP server, so you cannot natively send emails from your custom address through Cloudflare
  • No mailbox storage — there is no inbox within Cloudflare. Emails are forwarded and stored only at your destination (Gmail, Outlook, etc.)
  • SPF/DKIM considerations — forwarded emails may occasionally trigger spam filters at the destination. Cloudflare handles this well, but some edge cases exist
  • No calendar or contacts — this is email forwarding, not a full email suite like Google Workspace or Microsoft 365

For most small businesses and freelancers, these limitations are not deal-breakers — especially when you pair email routing with Gmail's "Send as" feature.

How to Send FROM Your Custom Address Using Gmail

Receiving emails at info@yourdomain.com is only half the equation. You also want to reply from that address so recipients see your custom domain, not your personal Gmail. Here is how to set it up:

  1. Open Gmail and go to Settings (gear icon) then See all settings
  2. Click the Accounts and Import tab
  3. Under "Send mail as", click "Add another email address"
  4. Enter your name and your custom email (e.g., info@yourdomain.com). Uncheck "Treat as an alias"
  5. For SMTP server, enter smtp.gmail.com, port 587, and use your Gmail credentials
  6. Gmail will send a confirmation code to your custom address. Since Cloudflare forwards it to your Gmail, you will receive it in the same inbox
  7. Enter the confirmation code, and you are done

Now when composing or replying to emails in Gmail, you can select your custom address in the "From" dropdown. You can even set it as the default so every email you send appears to come from your professional domain address.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Cloudflare Email Routing really free?
Yes, Cloudflare Email Routing is completely free for all Cloudflare plans, including the Free plan. There are no hidden costs, no per-address fees, and no message limits. You can create unlimited custom email addresses and forward them to any destination email at no charge.
Can I send emails from my custom domain address?
Cloudflare Email Routing only handles incoming mail forwarding — it does not support sending emails from your custom address natively. However, you can configure Gmail's "Send mail as" feature to send emails that appear to come from your custom domain address (e.g., info@yourdomain.com) using Gmail's SMTP servers for free.
How many custom email addresses can I create?
There is no limit. Cloudflare Email Routing allows you to create as many custom email addresses as you need — info@, support@, hello@, sales@, billing@, and any other prefix you want. You can also enable a catch-all rule to forward every possible address on your domain to a single destination.
