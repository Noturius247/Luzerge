What Is Always Online and Why Your Site Needs It

Luzerge Team March 16, 2026 4 min read

Imagine a potential customer visits your website at 2 AM. They want to learn about your services, check your pricing, or read your latest blog post. But instead of your polished homepage, they see a cold, white error page: "522 — Connection timed out." They close the tab, visit a competitor, and you never even know it happened.

Server outages are inevitable. Hardware fails. Hosting providers have bad days. Deployments go wrong. The question isn't whether your site will ever go down — it's what your visitors see when it does. That's where Cloudflare's Always Online feature comes in.

What Happens When Your Server Goes Down

When your origin server stops responding, Cloudflare normally returns an error page to visitors. Depending on the type of failure, they might see a 502 Bad Gateway, a 522 Connection Timed Out, or a 520 Web Server Error. None of these are good for business.

The real cost of downtime goes beyond the technical error code:

  • Lost trust — visitors who encounter an error page assume the business is unreliable or has shut down entirely
  • Lost revenue — every minute of downtime for an e-commerce site or lead generation page is money left on the table
  • SEO damage — if search engine crawlers hit your site during an outage, repeated 5xx errors can cause your pages to drop in rankings
  • Brand reputation — a single bad experience can turn away a customer permanently, especially if they're visiting for the first time

For small businesses in the Philippines that depend on their website for leads and orders, even a short outage during peak hours can have a measurable impact on the bottom line.

How Always Online Works Under the Hood

Cloudflare's Always Online feature acts as a safety net for your website. Here's what happens behind the scenes:

  1. Cloudflare crawls your site — periodically, Cloudflare's crawler (backed by the Internet Archive) takes snapshots of your website's pages. These snapshots are cached copies of your HTML content as it appeared at the time of the crawl.
  2. Your server goes down — when a visitor requests a page and Cloudflare detects that your origin server is unreachable (returning a 502, 521, 522, or 524 error), it intercepts the error.
  3. Cloudflare serves the cached snapshot — instead of showing the visitor an error page, Cloudflare serves the most recent cached version of the requested page. The visitor sees your content, your branding, and your messaging — not a blank error screen.
  4. A banner notifies the visitor — Cloudflare adds a small banner at the top of the page informing the visitor that they're viewing a cached version. This is transparent and honest, which actually builds trust.

The result is that your website appears to stay "online" even when your server is completely unreachable. Visitors can still browse your static content, read your blog posts, and see your contact information.

Always Online doesn't fix your server. It buys you time by keeping your front door open while you work on the problem behind the scenes.

How to Enable Always Online in Cloudflare

Turning on Always Online takes less than a minute. Here's how:

  1. Log into your Cloudflare dashboard and select the domain you want to configure
  2. Navigate to Caching > Configuration in the left sidebar
  3. Scroll down to the Always Online section
  4. Toggle the switch to On

That's it. Once enabled, Cloudflare will begin crawling and caching your site's pages. There's no additional configuration needed, and the feature is available on all Cloudflare plans, including the free tier.

Limitations of Always Online

Always Online is a powerful safety net, but it's not a magic solution. Understanding its limitations is crucial so you know what it can and can't do:

  • Static content only — Always Online serves cached HTML pages. It cannot process dynamic content like search queries, personalized dashboards, or real-time data feeds.
  • Forms don't work — contact forms, login pages, and signup flows require a live server to process submissions. Cached versions of these pages will display the form but won't be able to submit data.
  • E-commerce functionality breaks — shopping carts, checkout flows, and payment processing all require a live server connection. Visitors can view cached product pages, but they can't complete purchases.
  • Content may be stale — the cached version is a snapshot from the last crawl. If you updated your site recently, visitors might see an older version of your content during an outage.
  • Not all pages are cached — Cloudflare's crawler may not have reached every page on your site, especially deep or rarely linked pages. If a page wasn't cached, visitors will still see an error.
  • No real-time alerts — this is the biggest limitation. Always Online does not notify you that your server is down. It silently serves cached content, which means you might not realize there's a problem until the outage has been going on for hours.

When Always Online Isn't Enough

Here's the critical gap: Always Online protects the visitor experience, but it doesn't protect you. If your server goes down at midnight and comes back at 6 AM, Always Online will serve cached pages to visitors during that window. That's great for them. But you had no idea anything was wrong.

During those six hours, your contact forms weren't working. Your e-commerce checkout was broken. Your API endpoints were returning errors to any integrations depending on them. And you didn't find out until you checked your analytics the next morning and noticed a suspicious dip.

This is why uptime monitoring is not optional, even with Always Online enabled. Uptime monitoring does what Always Online cannot:

  • Detects outages immediately — monitoring services check your server every 60 seconds and alert you within minutes of a failure
  • Sends real-time alerts — get notified via email, SMS, or webhook so your team can start troubleshooting right away
  • Tracks response times — catch performance degradation before it becomes a full outage
  • Provides historical data — build a record of uptime and response times to identify patterns and hold your hosting provider accountable

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Cloudflare Always Online?
Cloudflare Always Online is a feature that serves a cached copy of your website to visitors when your origin server is unreachable. It uses snapshots taken by Cloudflare's crawler to display a static version of your pages, so visitors see content instead of an error page during an outage.
Does Always Online work for e-commerce sites?
Only partially. Always Online can display cached versions of static pages like your homepage and product listings, but it cannot process dynamic actions such as adding items to a cart, completing checkout, or logging into an account. E-commerce sites should pair Always Online with robust uptime monitoring to minimize actual downtime.
Is Always Online a replacement for uptime monitoring?
No. Always Online is a safety net that shows cached content during outages, but it does not alert you when your server goes down. Without uptime monitoring, you may not know your site is offline until customers complain. You need both: Always Online to protect the visitor experience and uptime monitoring to notify you so you can fix the root cause.
