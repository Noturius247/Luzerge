How Bot Fight Mode Protects Your Website from Automated Threats

Luzerge Team March 18, 2026 5 min read

According to recent industry reports, bots account for nearly half of all internet traffic. Not all of them are harmless. While some bots serve legitimate purposes — search engine crawlers indexing your pages, uptime monitors checking availability — a significant portion exists to exploit, scrape, and attack websites automatically and at scale.

If you run a business website, especially one that handles logins, e-commerce transactions, or user-generated content, automated bot traffic is not a distant threat. It is happening to your site right now. The question is whether you have the tools to detect and stop it.

What Are Bots and Why Are They a Problem?

A bot is any software program that performs automated tasks on the internet. While some bots are beneficial — like Googlebot indexing your content for search results — malicious bots are designed to exploit your website in several ways:

Credential Stuffing

Attackers use bots to test stolen username and password combinations against your login page at massive scale. They take credentials leaked from data breaches on other platforms and try them on your site, hoping users have reused passwords. A single bot can attempt thousands of login combinations per minute, and if even a small percentage succeed, the attacker gains access to real user accounts.

Content Scraping

Scraper bots copy your website content — product listings, pricing, articles, images — and republish it elsewhere. For businesses that invest in original content or maintain competitive pricing, this can undermine your market position. Scrapers also consume server resources, slowing your site down for legitimate visitors.

Inventory Hoarding

In e-commerce, bots can add items to shopping carts without completing purchases, effectively holding inventory hostage and preventing real customers from buying. This is especially damaging during sales events or limited product drops.

Spam and Form Abuse

Comment spam, fake form submissions, and automated account creation flood your site with junk data. This wastes your time moderating content, pollutes your analytics, and can even hurt your site's security posture by creating vectors for further attacks.

If you're not actively blocking malicious bots, you're essentially leaving your front door open and hoping nobody walks in.

How Cloudflare's Bot Fight Mode Works

Cloudflare's Bot Fight Mode is a free security feature available on all Cloudflare plans that automatically detects and mitigates malicious bot traffic. Unlike simple IP blocklists or user-agent filters, Bot Fight Mode uses a multi-layered approach to distinguish humans from bots:

Machine Learning-Based Detection

Cloudflare processes millions of requests per second across its global network. This massive dataset feeds machine learning models that can identify bot-like behavior patterns — even from bots that have never been seen before. The models analyze request characteristics such as TLS fingerprints, HTTP header ordering, and connection behavior to score each visitor's likelihood of being a bot.

JavaScript Challenges

When Bot Fight Mode suspects automated traffic, it serves a lightweight JavaScript challenge that runs in the visitor's browser. Legitimate browsers execute the challenge seamlessly and invisibly — real users never notice it. However, most bots lack full JavaScript execution environments, so they fail the challenge and are blocked. This approach is far less intrusive than CAPTCHAs while still being effective.

Behavioral Analysis

Bot Fight Mode examines how visitors interact with your site. Bots tend to exhibit patterns that differ from human behavior: they make requests at unnaturally consistent intervals, they don't load secondary resources like images or CSS, and they don't maintain cookies or session state in the way browsers do. By analyzing these behavioral signals across Cloudflare's entire network, the system can flag suspicious visitors in real time.

Types of Attacks Bot Fight Mode Blocks

Once enabled, Bot Fight Mode provides protection against a range of automated threats:

  • Credential stuffing attacks — automated login attempts using stolen credentials
  • Content scrapers — bots that copy your product data, pricing, or articles
  • Vulnerability scanners — automated tools probing your site for security weaknesses
  • Spam bots — automated form submissions and comment spam
  • DDoS bot participants — individual bots contributing to distributed denial of service attacks
  • Ad fraud bots — automated clicks on advertisements to drain your ad budget
  • Inventory scalping bots — automated purchasing or cart-holding scripts

For a broader view of how these threats fit into your overall security strategy, read our guide on why website security starts with monitoring.

How Bot Fight Mode Differs from Traditional WAF Rules

A Web Application Firewall (WAF) and Bot Fight Mode serve complementary but distinct roles in your security stack. Understanding the difference helps you deploy both effectively.

Traditional WAF rules work by inspecting the content of HTTP requests. They look for known attack patterns — SQL injection strings, cross-site scripting payloads, path traversal attempts — and block requests that match those signatures. You can learn more about what these headers reveal in our guide on how to read HTTP response headers.

Bot Fight Mode, by contrast, focuses on the identity of the requester. Instead of asking "Is this request malicious?", it asks "Is this visitor a human or a bot?" This distinction matters because:

  • WAF rules miss well-crafted bot requests — a bot performing credential stuffing sends perfectly normal-looking login requests. There's no malicious payload for the WAF to catch.
  • Bot Fight Mode misses payload-based attacks — a human attacker manually testing SQL injection would pass Bot Fight Mode because they are genuinely human.
  • Together, they cover both threat vectors — WAF blocks dangerous payloads regardless of who sends them, while Bot Fight Mode blocks automated actors regardless of what they send.

For comprehensive protection, you need both. Cloudflare enables WAF Managed Rules alongside Bot Fight Mode, and if you're using Luzerge to manage your Cloudflare setup, both features are configured as part of your security baseline.

Limitations: What Bot Fight Mode Doesn't Catch

Bot Fight Mode is powerful, but it's not a silver bullet. Understanding its limitations helps you build a more complete security strategy:

  • Sophisticated bots with real browsers — some advanced bots use headless Chrome or Firefox instances that can execute JavaScript challenges. These mimic human browsers closely enough to pass basic detection.
  • Targeted attacks by humans — Bot Fight Mode only addresses automated threats. A human attacker manually probing your site will pass through without issue.
  • API abuse with valid credentials — if an attacker has legitimate API keys or session tokens, their requests may look normal to Bot Fight Mode even if the usage pattern is abusive.
  • Low-and-slow attacks — bots that deliberately throttle their request rate to mimic human browsing patterns can sometimes evade detection.
  • False positives for unusual clients — some legitimate tools, custom HTTP clients, or accessibility software may occasionally be flagged as bot traffic.

To address these gaps, consider layering additional protections: rate limiting on sensitive endpoints, regular security scanning of your domain configuration, and upgrading to more advanced bot management when needed.

When to Upgrade to Super Bot Fight Mode

Cloudflare's free Bot Fight Mode provides solid baseline protection, but growing businesses may need more. Super Bot Fight Mode, available on Cloudflare Pro plans and above, adds several important capabilities:

  • Granular bot categories — distinguish between "definitely automated," "likely automated," and "likely human" traffic with separate actions for each category
  • JavaScript detection for static resources — extend bot detection to assets that the free tier doesn't cover
  • Custom bot responses — choose to challenge, block, or allow specific bot categories rather than applying a blanket policy
  • Analytics and visibility — detailed dashboards showing bot traffic trends, blocked request volumes, and bot score distributions

Consider upgrading if you experience any of the following:

  • Your site handles financial transactions or sensitive user data
  • You're seeing sophisticated bots that bypass the free tier's detection
  • You need detailed analytics on bot traffic patterns for compliance or reporting
  • You run an API that requires fine-grained access control

Malicious bots are not a future threat — they are hitting your website right now. Bot Fight Mode provides a free, effective first layer of defense that requires zero configuration expertise. Combined with proper website monitoring, WAF rules, and SSL management, it forms part of a comprehensive security strategy that keeps your site safe from automated exploitation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Bot Fight Mode?
Bot Fight Mode is a free Cloudflare security feature that automatically detects and blocks malicious bot traffic to your website. It uses machine learning, behavioral analysis, and JavaScript challenges to distinguish between legitimate visitors and automated threats like scrapers, credential stuffers, and spam bots.
Does Bot Fight Mode affect good bots like Googlebot?
No. Bot Fight Mode is designed to allow verified good bots like Googlebot, Bingbot, and other legitimate crawlers to access your site normally. Cloudflare maintains a list of verified bot IP ranges and user agents, so search engine indexing and other beneficial automated services are not affected.
Is Bot Fight Mode free?
Yes. Bot Fight Mode is available on all Cloudflare plans, including the free tier. You can enable it with a single toggle in your Cloudflare dashboard or through the Luzerge management panel. For more advanced bot management features, Cloudflare offers Super Bot Fight Mode on Pro plans and above.
What's the difference between Bot Fight Mode and WAF rules?
WAF (Web Application Firewall) rules protect against specific attack patterns like SQL injection and cross-site scripting by inspecting HTTP requests against known signatures. Bot Fight Mode focuses on identifying whether the visitor is a human or a bot using machine learning and behavioral analysis. They complement each other — WAF blocks malicious payloads while Bot Fight Mode blocks malicious actors.
